Card Delivery Redesign

The Challenge

Getting a card delivered is one of the first things a new T-Bank customer does — and how smooth that feels sets the tone for everything that follows. The old flow had gaps: no single screen to pick time and place, no clear explanation of the nameless card, and a long list of edge cases simply wasn't handled.

We needed to rebuild it: consolidate delivery setup into one screen, communicate the nameless card option clearly, and make sure every tricky situation — wrong address, unavailable slot, location not found — had a proper response.

Process

The designer and I started by mapping every existing state and error — laying out what the flow actually looked like before touching anything new. From there we worked through new states one by one.

One finding stood out: in user research, people associated "meeting" with something formal and effortful. "Delivery" felt quick and easy. So we switched to "delivery" throughout and documented it in our content guidelines.

Outcome

Time and place selection moved onto a single screen, cutting unnecessary steps. The nameless card option — delivered significantly faster — was explained clearly upfront, giving users a real choice.

Every edge case got a considered response. The language shift from "meeting" to "delivery" ran through the entire flow — a small word change that meaningfully reduced the perceived friction of the whole process.
